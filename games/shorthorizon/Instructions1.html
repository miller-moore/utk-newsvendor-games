In this experiment, you have the role of an inventory manager. You must decide how many units of a product you want to order and stock to sell to your customers.

You are making these decisions over multiple rounds. In each round, you are deciding on an order quantity.

You are making your decision under uncertainty. This means at the time of the order decision you do not know the exact demand of the period. However, you do know the probability distribution of the demand. The demand for your product will be shown by a distribution curve, shown on the decision-making page. Demand is independent between rounds.

You have no starting inventory in your warehouse at the beginning of the game. You will order which then get delivered before demand is realized. Any leftover inventory after demand is realized will be charged a salvage fee per unit left over.

{#      #} For example:

  • Available inventory: 90 units
  • Demand: 80 units
  • Leftover inventory: 90-80=10 units

Salvage fees are charged for the resulting leftover 10 units that are stocked in inventory.

If you purchased too few units in a period to fulfill this periodâ€™s demand, the unfulfilled demand is lost. You cannot reorder within a period or shift demand to later periods. For example:

  • Purchased inventory: 30 units
  • Demand: 80 units
  • Units sold: 30 units

You are trying to maximize your profits in this experiment. Profit per round is calculated as follows:

Profit per round = selling price x units sold - purchasing price x order quantity - salvage fee * leftover inventory

Therefore, profit = revenue - purchase costs - salvage costs.

In this experiment, your costs will be displayed on the decision page.
